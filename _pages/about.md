We build generalizable, uncertainty-aware “world models” for tumor evolution and metastasis, models that 
- learn causal/functional links from genotype to regulatory state to phenotype, 
- explicitly represent measurement noise and batch effects in real sequencing data, and 
- transfer across cancers, tissues, and platforms.

The scientific payoff is how somatic/germline variation rewires regulatory programs, how early genetic events bias invasive and metastatic routes, and how chromosomal instability (CIN) and tumor-intrinsic programs enable organ-specific colonization. 

The translational payoff is cheaper, scalable profiling and cleaner data, enabling robust biomarkers and therapeutic hypotheses.
