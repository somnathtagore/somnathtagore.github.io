Metastasis is responsible for the majority of cancer-related deaths, yet the biological programs that determine where tumors spread remain poorly understood.

In this study, we analyzed large-scale **single-cell transcriptomic data from pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC)** to identify tumor cell states associated with metastatic organotropism. By integrating transcriptional programs with clinical outcomes, we uncovered cellular states that correlate with both metastatic patterns and patient survival.

These findings suggest that metastatic behavior is not solely determined by genetic alterations, but also by the transcriptional states adopted by tumor cells. Distinct cellular programs appear to predispose tumors to colonize specific organs and influence disease progression.

The work provides new insight into the relationship between tumor cell plasticity, metastatic dissemination, and clinical outcomes in PDAC.

**Journal:** Nature Genetics (2025)
