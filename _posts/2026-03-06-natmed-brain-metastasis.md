This post highlights our recent work on non-small cell lung cancer brain metastasis.

Using multi-modal single-cell and spatial profiling, we found that brain metastases are characterized by pronounced chromosomal instability and neural-like transcriptional programs. Rather than simply reconstructing tumor evolution, this study focused on identifying biological features that distinguish metastatic tumors from primary lesions and may help explain metastatic fitness in the brain microenvironment.

Key themes from this work include:
- chromosomal instability as a major feature of metastatic biology,
- neural-like state programs in metastatic tumor cells,
- integration of single-cell and spatial transcriptomic data,
- implications for biomarker discovery and therapeutic targeting.
