Pan-cancer inference and validation of hypermorphic, hypomorphic and neomorphic mutations

March 7, 2026

2026   ·   cancer genomics   functional mutations   pan-cancer   computational biology     ·   research  

Mutations in cancer genes do not all behave the same way. While many are broadly classified as gain-of-function or loss-of-function, the functional consequences of mutations are often more nuanced.

In this work, we developed a computational framework to systematically infer hypermorphic, hypomorphic, and neomorphic mutations across diverse cancer types. By integrating large-scale genomic and transcriptomic datasets, we identified mutations that increase gene activity, partially reduce function, or generate entirely new molecular behavior.

The study combines computational prediction with experimental validation to demonstrate that these functional mutation classes can reshape regulatory networks in distinct ways. Our findings highlight the importance of moving beyond simple binary classifications of mutations and instead recognizing the diverse spectrum of functional effects that mutations can produce.

Understanding these mutation classes provides a more precise framework for interpreting cancer genomes and may help guide therapeutic strategies that target specific functional alterations.

Journal: Nature Genetics (2026)
